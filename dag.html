The DAG Observatory

The Observatory

The Doğu Anadolu Gözlemevi (Eastern Anatolia Observatory, abbreviated as DAG) is the new Turkish national observatory, located on the Karakaya Ridge close to the city of Erzurum at an elevation of 3170 m above sea level.

A clear nights ratio of 260/365 and a median seeing of 0.9" provides promising conditions for observations with the PLACID coronagraph (see plot for on-site measurements).

The Telescope

The telescope of the observatory has a primary mirror of 4m in diameter, collecting all the light required for the PLACID instrument, which will be installed at the diffraction limited Nasmyth platform. This means it will be located downstream of the telescope's extreme adaptive optics system (XAO), called TROIA.

Partners

The project has been realized thanks to and in close collaboration with HEIG-VD in Yverdon-les-Bains, Switzerland and the ATASAM institute at Atatürk University, Erzurum, Turkey. AMOS and EIE are the contractors responsible for the construction of the observatory and the telescope.
