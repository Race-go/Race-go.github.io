Research

1) PLACID Commissioning and Early Science

This section is focused on the commissioning and early science phases of PLACID (Programmable Liquid-crystal Active Coronagraphic Imager for the DAG telescope) project development. This section outlines the objectives, challenges, and preliminary results of the PLACID project as it begins to take shape.

SPIE Proceeding: Discovery space and science with the PLACID stellar coronagraph

SPIE Poster: Discovery space and science with the PLACID stellar coronagraph

2) Understanding Discrete Pixelated Phase Coronagraphy with SLMs

This section delves into the fundamentals of discrete pixelated phase coronagraphy using Spatial Light Modulators (SLMs). The focus is on understanding the mechanics, applications, and advantages of this technology in high-contrast imaging systems.

SPIE Proceeding: Future exoplanet direct imaging instruments: Simulating spatial light modulator-based pixelated focal-plane coronagraphy

SPIE Poster: Future exoplanet direct imaging instruments: Simulating spatial light modulator-based pixelated focal-plane coronagraphy

3) Coherent Differential Imaging (CDI)

Coherent Differential Imaging (CDI) is a cutting-edge technique used to enhance image contrast and suppress noise in exoplanet direct imaging observations. This section explores the principles behind CDI, its implementation, and its potential impact on direct imaging.
